Young Family Planning

At Ross Estate Law we love working with growing families to develop comprehensive estate plans that can ensure your family and assets are not just protected but growing. Working closely with financial planners we help you develop a plan that will build your family's wealth over time. The younger you start the better!



Estate planning isn't just for the elderly or the wealthy—it's something everyone, especially young people and young parents, should consider. While thinking about what happens to your assets, medical care, and legal decisions might feel like a distant concern, having an estate plan in place early can protect your future and the well-being of your loved ones. By starting to plan now, you are benefitting you and your family in the long run.

If you are in a common law relationship, Ross Estate Law can ensure your relationship is respected and your wishes are followed. If you have children, Ross Estate Law can work with you to create a guardianship agreement that will keep your children safe and well cared for.

At Ross Estate Law we are enthusiastic to work with LBGTQ+ couples, families with members transitioning, and nontraditional family forms. We are attuned to the particular issues that can arise with queer families and are perfectly positioned to help.

1. Control Over Decisions

Having an estate plan means you can control decisions about your financial affairs, medical care, and the distribution of your assets, even if you are unable to make these decisions yourself. Whether it's due to an accident, illness, or incapacity, a will, power of attorney, and medical directive ensure that your wishes are respected.

2. Legal Protection

An estate plan provides legal protection, ensuring your assets are managed according to your preferences and that your medical and financial decisions are clearly defined. Without such documents, families may face confusion and conflict during tough times. Having a clear estate plan can help prevent family disputes and ensure that your loved ones are protected legally.

3. Compounding Growth

By starting to save and invest early, young families can take advantage of the power of compounding growth. The earlier you start, the greater the potential for your wealth to grow over time, providing you with more financial security for your family's future.

4. Financial Security

Having a clear estate plan helps ensures that your family has financial security, no matter what the future holds.

5. Education and Future Planning

Early estate planning helps young parents save for significant future expenses, such as their children's education. By managing your finances early, you can ensure that your children have the resources they need when the time comes for higher education, providing a foundation for their future success.

Estate planning isn't just something for later in life—it's a crucial step for young people and young parents who want to ensure their wishes are respected, provide for their children, and secure their family's financial future. Starting early provides significant benefits, including greater wealth accumulation, financial security, and a clear plan for managing life's uncertainties.

Whether you're just starting your career or growing your family, taking the time to create a will, designate a power of attorney, and establish a medical directive will pay off in the long run, providing peace of mind for you and your loved ones.
