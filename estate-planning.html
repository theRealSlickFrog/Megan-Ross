Estate Planning
in Halifax, Nova Scotia

At Ross Estate Law, we believe estate planning is more than just drafting documents—it's about building peace of mind and protecting your family's future. Based in Halifax and serving clients throughout Nova Scotia, we help individuals and families create comprehensive estate plans that reflect their values, secure their assets, and ensure their wishes are respected.

Estate planning isn't only for the elderly or the wealthy. Whether you are a young professional, a parent of growing children, or approaching retirement, having an estate plan in place ensures your loved ones are cared for and your decisions are clear. From wills and trusts to powers of attorney and personal care directives, we guide you through every step with clarity and compassion.

Our estate planning services include:

• Drafting wills that clearly outline how your assets will be distributed.

• Creating trusts to protect and grow family wealth.

• Establishing powers of attorney for financial and medical decision-making.

• Preparing personal care directives to guide healthcare choices.

• Coordinating with financial planners to align your estate with long-term goals.

An example:

Imagine a couple in their early 40s, raising two children in Halifax, with a mortgage, some savings, and modest investments. They assume estate planning is something to deal with later in life. However, if one parent were suddenly unable to make decisions due to illness or accident, their family could face significant legal and financial challenges. By working with Ross Estate Law, they are able to create wills, name guardians for their children, set up powers of attorney, and prepare healthcare directives—ensuring their children are protected and decisions are made according to their wishes.

Why Nova Scotians choose Ross Estate Law:

• Local expertise in Halifax and across Nova Scotia's estate laws.

• A personalized, step-by-step process to simplify complex decisions.

• Compassionate guidance that reduces stress and family conflict.

• A focus on protecting both your wealth and your loved ones' well-being.

Estate planning isn't something to put off until later—it's an essential part of protecting your family at every stage of life. Whether you're just starting a career, raising a family, or planning for retirement, Ross Estate Law will help you build a plan that grows with you.
